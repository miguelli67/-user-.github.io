Biology

Biology is the study of living organisms and their interactions with each other and their environments. This course covers:

  • Cell structure and function: the differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, and the roles of organelles like the nucleus, mitochondria and ribosomes.
  • Genetics: how DNA encodes information, basic Mendelian inheritance, and the structure of chromosomes.
  • Evolution and natural selection: the mechanisms that drive species change over time.
  • Ecology: ecosystems, food webs, and the flow of energy and nutrients.
  • Human anatomy: overview of major organ systems and their functions.

Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Cells

Prokaryotic cells (like bacteria) do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Eukaryotic cells (like plant and animal cells) have a defined nucleus and organelles such as mitochondria and endoplasmic reticulum.
