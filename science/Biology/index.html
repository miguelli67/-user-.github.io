  • Cell structure and function: the differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, and the roles of organelles like the nucleus, mitochondria and ribosomes.

Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Cells

Prokaryotic cells (like bacteria) do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Eukaryotic cells (like plant and animal cells) have a defined nucleus and organelles such as mitochondria and endoplasmic reticulum.

Common Organelles

  • Nucleus: Stores DNA and controls cell activity.
  • Mitochondria: Produces energy (ATP) through cellular respiration.
  • Ribosomes: Build proteins from amino acids.
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum: Helps transport materials inside the cell.
  • Golgi Apparatus: Packages and ships proteins.
  • Cell Membrane: Controls what enters and leaves the cell.
